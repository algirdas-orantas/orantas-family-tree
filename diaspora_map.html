🇱🇹 Lithuanian Origins — Suvalkija

Kybartai / Bijotai / Naumiestis cluster
All Orantas emigrants to Brazil originate from a 15 km radius in southwestern Lithuania (Suvalkija region, Vilkaviškio apskritis). Two separate Jonas Orantas families lived in this area:

■ Jonas #1 — Kybartai town (d. 1926). Children: Antanas → Brazil, Anelė → stayed.
■ Jonas #2 — Bijotai village (alive 1939). Children: Vicente → Brazil, Justinas → stayed, Jonas Jr. → stayed.
Deep roots: Gelgaudiškis Orintas clan
The Orintas surname is recorded in church books since the 1780s in Gelgaudiškis parish (Šakiai), ~25 km north of Kybartai. Four generations traced: Jonas Orintas (c.1787) → Baltramiejus → Vincas → Jonas (m.1932). The spelling variation Orintas/Orantas/Orentas (vowel i/a/e) is confirmed to occur within the same families.

🌊 Emigration Context — Why Brazil?

Pre-war economic emigration (1929–1930)
The Orantas emigration to Brazil was part of a pre-war economic wave, distinct from the later WWII/DP camp refugees. The Bremen passenger list shows ~18 Lithuanian emigrants on the same ship (SS Sierra Cordoba, Jan 1930), all listed as agricultural laborers heading to Santos — an organized group emigration.

Lithuania in the late 1920s was a poor agricultural country. Suvalkija (southwestern Lithuania) had particularly small landholdings. Brazil was actively recruiting European agricultural workers. Lithuanian communities already existed in São Paulo (Bom Retiro, Vila Zelina).
Three fates from Kybartai
Three Orantas men from the same ~15 km area, three very different fates:

● Vincas (b.1908) — emigrated 1929/30 as pre-war economic emigrant directly to São Paulo. Became foundry worker. Died 1983.

● Antanas (b.1901) — emigrated ~1929 (date uncertain). Insurance agent → laborer. Died 1947 age 46.

● Estanislau (b.1911) — emigrated before 1935. Cook. Parentage uncertain (which Jonas?).

Meanwhile, their siblings who stayed — Justinas, Jonas Jr., Anelė — lived through Soviet occupation, WWII, and the re-occupation of Lithuania. Justinas died in 1970 Virbalis; Jonas Jr. in 1986 Kybartai.
The São Paulo Lithuanian colony
São Paulo had one of the largest Lithuanian communities in South America. Key areas:

Vila Zelina — the heart of the Lithuanian community, sometimes called "Little Lithuania." Vicente lived here.
Bom Retiro — immigrant receiving neighborhood. First address for many arrivals.

The community was tight-knit enough that:
• Registrars confused one family's wife with another's
• Lithuanian-language obituaries were still published in the 1980s
• Children born in Brazil received Lithuanian names (Birutė)
• Wedding witnesses were fellow Lithuanians (Magunavičius)
